## Cinema
*Films & TV*

### Come and See
*1985 · Klimov*

The most visceral anti-war film ever made. The final scene dismantles everything. I've never recovered.

### Mulholland Drive
*2001 · Lynch*

A film about the gap between what we imagine our lives to be and what they are.

### Shoplifters
*2018 · Kore-eda*

The gentlest demolition of the idea of family I've ever seen.

### The Great Escape
*1963 · Sturges*

Peak ensemble filmmaking. Still holds up completely.

## Games
*Video & Tabletop*

### Bloodborne
*2015 · FromSoftware*

The atmosphere is unmatched. Everything about it — the music, the lore, the creature design — coheres into something genuinely disquieting.

### Elden Ring
*2022 · FromSoftware*

The open world finally clicked for me on the second playthrough. The best map design in any game.

### Metal Gear Solid
*1998 · Kojima*

Formative. I think about the Codec conversations more than most conversations I've had in real life.

## Books
*Fiction & Non-fiction*

### Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance
*1974 · Pirsig*

The argument about Quality is more interesting than the motorcycle journey, but you need the motorcycle journey.

### Underworld
*1997 · DeLillo*

The most ambitious American novel of the last fifty years. The prologue alone.

### The Road
*2005 · McCarthy*

Formally perfect. The prose is doing everything the story needs and nothing it doesn't.

## Music

### What's Going On
*1971 · Marvin Gaye*

The album I've returned to more times than any other. Completely whole.

### Sign 'O' the Times
*1987 · Prince*

The breadth of it is staggering. Two records, no filler, complete command of every genre he touches.

### Is This It
*2001 · The Strokes*

I know it's a cliché but I was fifteen and it changed my understanding of what rock music could sound like.
