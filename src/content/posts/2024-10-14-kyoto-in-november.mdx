November is the correct time to be in Kyoto. Every other month is preparation for or recovery from November^(The rainy season in June makes the moss gardens extraordinary, but November wins on overall aesthetic coherence.).

The maple trees turn a particular shade of red that has no real equivalent in the English color vocabulary. Not scarlet. Not crimson. Something closer to the color of old lacquer, a red that has been handled and darkened by time.

I arrived at Saihō-ji before the gates opened and stood outside listening to monks chanting. The sound came through the wall in a way that felt architectural — the garden itself seemed to be participating in it.
