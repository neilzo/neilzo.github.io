Every year or two I audit what's actually running on my machine versus what I think I use. The gap is always instructive.

**Daily drivers:**

*Obsidian* for notes. The local-first, plain-text approach is the right call for anything you want to exist in ten years. I have notes from 2016 that open cleanly today^(Try opening a 2016 Evernote export and see how far you get.). The plugin ecosystem is good but I use maybe four of them.

*Raycast* replaced Spotlight and then Alfred for me. The calculator, clipboard history, and window management alone justify it.

*Reeder* for RSS. I still believe in RSS. The chronological feed of sources I've chosen is qualitatively different from any algorithmic alternative.

**Things I have tried and abandoned:**

Linear (too much structure for solo work), Notion (the block model is clever until it isn't), Things (beautiful but the capture friction got to me), every Pomodoro timer app (I just use a kitchen timer).

**The interesting category:** tools that do one thing and don't try to become platforms. An app that solves a specific problem and then gets out of your way is a gift.
