## General

### Begs the question
It’s really a logical fallacy. People should use “raises the question” instead.

### Air fryer
There’s no frying. It’s a convention oven.

### Homogeneous
It's not "ha-ma-gen-ous" it's "homo-gene-e-ous". You don’t say the opposite, heterogeneous, in the former way.

### Exposed brick
Unnecessary real estate marketing. I know it's sexier than "brick wall" but damn.

### Literally
People don’t use the original meaning, but I’ve long accepted this battle is lost.

## Corporate talk

### Flywheel
Unnecessary business buzzword and no one knows what it actually is. I prefer "positive feedback loop" since it's more direct and easily understandable.

### Saying “giving time back” if a meeting ends early
You don’t have or own my time, thank you very much!

### Learnings
We already have a word for this, and it’s called “lessons”.

### “Are you free for a quick 30m chat?”
Since when is 30 minutes quick? Just drop the quick part 😭

### Saying “quick question”
Often the question isn’t quick nor is the response. Why not just say “I have a question” or “question”?

### People saying they’re excited at work when they’re really not.
E.g. “I’m excited to show, we’re excited to announce, this X work thing is really exciting”, etc. Frequently accompanied by the flatest tone.

### “Would you mind…” when asking someone to do a core job function
There's other ways you can be polite when asking someone to do their job. How about "Could you..." or "When you have a chance..."?
